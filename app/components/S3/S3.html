Mastering the creative distraction technique

  • The really good news is that you can start to practise and master this technique right now.
  • Here is one way of learning creative distraction, sit quietly and with minimal bodily movement. Allow yourself to relax.
  • Turn your attention outward to things around you, carefully look around and, in a calm and steady fashion, observing 4 different objects. Then describe each object to yourself in short simple sentences. Say what you actually see
    1. "I see the light, yellow coloured outside edge of the lower-most leaf of the plant on the table"
    2. "I see the grey shading of my right sock just below my trouser leg".
  • Do this without haste and in a smooth, continuous way
  • Next turn your attention towards your auditory senses, notice what it is you are hearing, the sounds around you.
    1. You may hear the gentle rustling sound of the motor that drives your computer ventilator.
    2. Take notice also of where that sound is coming from.
    3. “It is coming from the lower right of my body”
    4. It can be helpful to say what it is you are hearing and where it is.
    5. “I can hear a blackbird singing outside.”
  • After a short break of about 30 seconds, move on to the sensations of touch, take note of any physical feelings,
    1. the different textures of your clothes
    2. the pressure of your body against a chair
    3. the slight numbing around your right hand when it is perched above the computer keyboard.
  • Another method is to picture the goal you are trying to achieve if unwanted or negative thoughts enter your mind, if you are thinking about eating chocolate, then concentrate on seeing yourself slimmer and thinking about how you will feel when you reach your desired weight.
